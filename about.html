About This Dashboard

This dashboard examines the public record of formal biological opinions issued by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (USFWS) under Section 7 of the Endangered Species Act. It demonstrates that the publicly available data in the ECOSPHERE system is a small, inconsistent subset of actual ESA consultations — making year-over-year trends unreliable and public accountability difficult.

USFWS logs all consultations in its internal Tracking and Integrated Logging System (TAILS), which records roughly 935 formal biological opinions per year. The public ECOSPHERE system captures only 300–500 of those annually, depending on voluntary data entry by field staff. The analysis quantifies this gap and highlights the data quality problems in detail.

Data is compiled from the USFWS ECOSPHERE Biological Opinion Reports. The TAILS benchmark is drawn from Malcom & Li (2015), PNAS.
